üéØ FIRE Movement Strategies

The Financial Independence Retire Early (FIRE) movement offers multiple paths to early retirement. Choose the strategy that aligns with your lifestyle and financial goals.

Lean FIRE

Retire early with a minimalist lifestyle, typically requiring $25,000-40,000 per year. Focus on reducing expenses and living frugally to achieve financial independence faster.

Traditional FIRE

Accumulate 25x your annual expenses using the 4% rule. Maintain your current lifestyle in retirement with balanced savings and spending approach.

Fat FIRE

Retire with a comfortable lifestyle requiring $100,000+ per year. Accumulate larger nest egg for luxury retirement without financial constraints.

Coast FIRE

Save aggressively early, then let investments grow without additional contributions. Work part-time or in lower-stress jobs while investments compound to retirement age.

Barista FIRE

Achieve partial financial independence, then work part-time for health insurance and supplemental income. Reduces retirement savings needed while maintaining flexibility.

üìä Investment & Retirement Resources

Essential retirement planning knowledge and investment strategies to accelerate your path to financial independence.

The 4% Rule

Withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually in retirement. Multiply your annual expenses by 25 to calculate your FIRE number. Example: $40,000/year expenses = $1,000,000 needed.

Index Fund Investing

Low-cost index funds (like VTSAX or VTI) offer broad market exposure with minimal fees. Historical 7-10% annual returns make them ideal for long-term wealth building.

Tax-Advantaged Accounts

Maximize 401k ($23,500 limit), IRA ($7,000 limit), and HSA ($4,150 limit) contributions. Tax savings accelerate wealth building significantly over decades.

Asset Allocation

Diversify between stocks, bonds, and real estate. Common rule: subtract your age from 110 for stock percentage. Rebalance annually to maintain target allocation.

Emergency Fund

Maintain 6-12 months expenses in high-yield savings account. Provides security buffer and prevents tapping retirement accounts during emergencies.

üí° Essential Financial Concepts

Compound Interest: The 8th Wonder

$10,000 invested at 7% annual return grows to $76,123 in 30 years. Time in the market beats timing the market. Start investing early to maximize compound growth.

Savings Rate Matters Most

50% savings rate means financial independence in 17 years. 75% savings rate achieves FIRE in just 7 years. Your savings rate determines your timeline more than investment returns.

Lifestyle Inflation is the Enemy

Resist increasing spending as income rises. Banking raises and bonuses dramatically accelerates FIRE timeline. Live below your means to achieve financial freedom faster.
